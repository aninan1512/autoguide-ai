Battery Diagnosis Guide

Common Symptoms

* Car will not start
* Clicking sound when turning the key
* Dashboard lights dim or flicker
* Electrical accessories not working

Possible Causes

* Dead battery
* Corroded battery terminals
* Loose battery cables
* Old battery (typically older than 3–5 years)
* Faulty alternator not charging the battery

Diagnostic Steps

1. Check battery voltage with a multimeter. A healthy battery should read about 12.6 volts when the engine is off.
2. Inspect battery terminals for corrosion or loose connections.
3. Try jump-starting the vehicle.
4. Test the alternator output with the engine running (around 13.7–14.7 volts).
5. Replace the battery if it cannot hold a charge.

Safety Notes

* Always disconnect the negative terminal first.
* Wear protective gloves when handling battery terminals.

When to visit a mechanic

* If the alternator is suspected to be faulty.
* If electrical issues continue after replacing the battery.
